History & Philosophy

Born from Resilience,
Guided by Wonder

The Reggio Emilia approach originated in Northern Italy after World War II. Loris Malaguzzi, an educator and visionary, partnered with families in the town of Reggio Emilia who wanted to rebuild their community â€” starting with their children. Together, they built schools grounded in respect, collaboration, and the belief that children are naturally curious and capable learners.

At Little Explorers, we carry that spirit forward. We believe children communicate through what Malaguzzi called the "Hundred Languages" â€” drawing, building, dramatic play, movement, and more. Our role as educators is to listen, document, and respond to each child's unique ways of making meaning.

Our theoretical foundation draws from constructivist theorists like Jean Piaget (children build knowledge through experience), Lev Vygotsky (learning is social and language-rich), and John Dewey (learning by doing). These principles shape everything we do.
